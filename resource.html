Government Schemes

Discover subsidies and programs for farmers. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN): A central sector scheme that provides a financial benefit of Rs 6,000 per year to small and marginal farmers. The money is transferred to the farmers' bank accounts in three equal installments every four months. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY): A scheme that provides a monthly pension of Rs 3,000 to small and marginal farmers who are between 18 and 40 years old and have up to 2 hectares of cultivable land. The pension is paid once the farmer reaches 60 years of age. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY): A scheme for farmers. Modified Interest Subvention Scheme (MISS): A scheme for farmers. Urea Subsidy Scheme: A scheme that ensures a constant supply of urea to farmers at a price of Rs 242 per 45 kg bag.

The Indian Journal of Agricultural Sciences A journal devoted to experimental agriculture and abstracted by all the major abstracting services. It includes articles on cytology, genetics, breeding, agronomy, soil science, horticulture, water use, microbiology, plant diseases and pest, agricultural engineering, economics and statistics with emphasis on original articles, from India and countries having similar agricultural conditions.
